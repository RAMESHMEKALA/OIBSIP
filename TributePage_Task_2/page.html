APJ Abdul Kalam: The Missile Man of India

About APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a scientist, educator, and statesman. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's missile defense program and is widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India." His contributions to science and technology, particularly in the field of aerospace engineering, earned him numerous accolades and awards.

Despite his towering achievements, Dr. Kalam remained humble and dedicated to the welfare of his fellow citizens, especially the youth. He was a fervent advocate for education and believed in the power of knowledge to transform lives.
